Why Remove a Tree?

When Tree Removal Is the Right Call

Tree removal is sometimes the safest and most responsible decision a property owner can make. Whether a tree is dead, diseased, structurally compromised, or positioned too close to a structure, leaving it in place can create serious hazards — especially during the high-wind storms and fire seasons common throughout Grass Valley, Auburn, and the surrounding Nevada County foothills.

At Powell Arbor Solutions, every tree removal begins with a thorough assessment by ISA Certified Arborist David Powell. We evaluate the tree's health, structural integrity, proximity to structures and utilities, and the safest removal approach for your specific site. We never recommend removal unless it's genuinely necessary — sometimes the right answer is strategic trimming or a crown reduction instead.

When removal is the right course, our experienced crew executes the work safely, cleanly, and efficiently — minimizing impact on your property and leaving the site clean. We handle everything from the initial cut to final debris removal, with optional stump grinding to complete the job.

Common Questions

Tree Removal FAQs

Answers to the questions we hear most from homeowners and property managers across Nevada and Placer Counties.

Tree removal costs vary based on tree size, species, location, and access. A small tree under 30 feet typically ranges from $300–$700, while large pines or oaks over 60 feet near structures can run $1,200–$3,000+. We provide detailed written estimates at no charge — contact us and we'll assess your specific situation in person.

Permit requirements vary by jurisdiction and tree type. In the City of Grass Valley, permits may be required for certain tree species or sizes in the public right-of-way. In unincorporated Nevada County, most private property removals do not require a permit unless you're in a specific overlay zone. We're familiar with local requirements and can help you understand what applies to your property.

Coverage depends on your policy and the circumstances. If a tree falls and damages a structure, many homeowner's policies cover the removal of that tree as part of the damage claim. Elective removal of a standing tree — even a dead or hazardous one — is typically not covered. We recommend contacting your insurer for specifics. We're experienced working alongside insurance claims when applicable.

Most residential tree removals are completed in a single day. Small to medium trees can be removed in a few hours. Large or complex removals — particularly trees near structures, with limited access, or requiring extensive rigging — may take a full day or occasionally two. We'll give you an accurate time estimate during the on-site assessment.

Yes — stump grinding is available as an add-on service. We grind stumps below grade so you can replant or reclaim the area. If you're not ready to grind immediately, we can return at a later date. Just let us know during the estimate and we'll include it in your quote.
