Common Questions

Arborist Report & Consultation FAQs

Answers to the questions we hear most from homeowners, property managers, and real estate professionals across Placer and Nevada County.

An arborist report is a formal written document prepared by a certified arborist that assesses the condition, health, risk, or value of one or more trees. They're commonly requested for permit applications, insurance claims, real estate transactions, construction projects near trees, HOA disputes, and any situation where an independent professional opinion on tree condition is needed. If you're making a significant decision based on the status of trees on your property, an arborist report gives you defensible, documented support for that decision.

Reports prepared by ISA Certified Arborists are the standard accepted by most insurance carriers for tree-related claims and inspections. David Powell's ISA Certification (#WE-8944A) and California Contractor's License (#999635) meet the professional credentials most insurers require. We recommend confirming your insurer's specific requirements before scheduling — but in most cases, an ISA Certified Arborist report is exactly what they're asking for.

Yes — and many buyers throughout Grass Valley, Nevada City, Auburn, and rural Nevada County are now including arborist inspections as part of their due diligence, especially on properties with large pines, oaks, or trees near structures. A pre-purchase assessment identifies hazardous trees, estimates maintenance needs, and flags potential liabilities before you take ownership. It's a relatively small investment that can prevent very large surprises after closing.

Construction — including grading, trenching, foundation work, and driveway installation — can severely damage or kill trees through root injury and soil compaction, often without any immediate visible symptoms. We assess which trees are at risk based on their proximity to planned activity, root architecture, and species tolerance. We also establish tree protection zones and can prepare written tree protection plans for submission with building permits when required by local jurisdictions.

Absolutely — and we encourage it whenever you have doubt about a recommendation to remove a tree. Not all contractors who do tree work are certified arborists, and even among certified professionals, opinions can differ. David will walk your property, independently assess the trees in question, and give you an honest evaluation of whether removal is genuinely warranted, whether alternative treatments exist, and what the realistic risk level is if you choose to wait or preserve the tree.

An on-site consultation typically takes 30 minutes to two hours depending on the number of trees and the complexity of the assessment. Written reports are generally delivered within a few business days of the site visit. If you have a time-sensitive situation — such as a closing deadline or pending insurance inspection — let us know upfront and we'll do our best to accommodate your timeline.
